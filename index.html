Intentions of Vote - Chile Dictatorship Referendum 1989

National survey conducted in April and May of 1988 by FLACSO , The Latinamerican Faculty of Social Sciences, a UNESCO initiative created in 1957

This graph represents visually the Voting intentions in the Referendum that would determine if Chile would continue under Dictatorship ('Yes') or return to democracy ('No').

It is important to remember that people contrary to the regime did not feel at liberty of expressing political opinion

From the above data we can see that, those who said would vote 'No' were overwhelmingly from Santiago, the capital of the Country, and had a Postsecundary Education.

We can also observe Males were more likely to say they would vote 'No', while Females were most likely to be undecided.

Larger incomes are related to a 'Yes' vote. This is explained because of the positive Economic implications Dictatorship had for the country.

It is vital to remember that the United States had strong blocks and isolation on the previous government in the context of Cold War.

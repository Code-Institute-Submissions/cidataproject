Intentions of Vote - Chile Dictatorship Referendum 1989

National survey conducted in April and May of 1988 by FLACSO , The Latinamerican Faculty of Social Sciences, a UNESCO initiative created in 1957

This graph visually represents the Voting intentions in the Referendum that would determine if Chile would continue under Dictatorship ('Yes') or return to democracy ('No'). Select the Vote you wish to study to modify the graphs below

It is important to remember that people contrary to the regime did not feel at liberty of expressing political opinion

From the above data we can observe that Males were more likely to intended to vote 'No', while most Females surveyed claimed they would vote 'Yes'. Females were also more likely than men to be Undecided or to Abstain altogether from the Referendum.

We can also observe how those who intended to vote 'No' were overwhelmingly from Santiago, the capital of the Country.

A correlation between a Postsecondary Education and a 'No' vote can be observed. Universities at the same time are highly concentrated in the capital.

Larger incomes are related to a 'Yes' vote. This is explained because of the positive Economic implications Dictatorship had for the country, mostly due to the Economic sanctions imposed by the United States on the previous socialist Government of Salvador Allende, in the context of the Cold War.

Older respondants were less likely to declare they would vote 'No' when compared with younger ones. 'Yes' votes were however not exclusive of elder generations.

Intentions of Vote - Chile Dictatorship Plebiscite 1989

National survey conducted in April and May of 1988 by FLACSO/Chile.

FLACSO (Facultad Latinoamericana de Ciencias Sociales/Latinamerican Faculty of Social Sciences) is a UNESCO initiative created in 1957.

This graph represents visually the Voting intentions in the Plebiscite that would determine if Chile would continue under Dictatorship ('Yes') or return to democracy ('No'). Other options are A for"Abstain" and U for "Undecided".

It is important to remember that people contrary to the regime did not feel at liberty of expressing political opinion

Choose from the graph above the Vote you wish to study to modify the graphs beneath

From the above data we can see that, those who said would vote 'No' were overwhelmingly from Santiago, the capital of the Country. We can also observe Males were more likely to say they would vote 'No', while Females were most likely to be undecided. Larger incomes are also related to a 'Yes' vote. This is explained because of the positive Economic implications Dictatorship had for the country. It is vital to remember that the United States had strong blocks and isolation on the previous government in the context of Cold War.

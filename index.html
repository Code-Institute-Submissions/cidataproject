Chile Dictatorship Referendum

Intentions of Vote

This project

The following graphs visually represent Voting intentions for the 1988 Referendum that would determine if Chile would continue under Dictatorship ('Yes') or return to democracy ('No')

The data was obtained from the National survey conducted in April and May of 1988 by FLACSO The Latin American Faculty of Social Sciences, a UNESCO initiative created in 1957 [1]

Under each graphic you will find some discussion and context on the findings. This information will aid in the understanding of the presented data.

This discussion does not intend to be fully comprehensive, since an in-depth analisys of the displayed data is not the objective in this opportunity. [2]

Sources are cited when needed.

It is important to remember that people contrary to the regime did not feel at liberty of expressing political opinion

Gender - Region

In the above charts we can observe Males were more likely to prefer 'No', while most Females surveyed claimed they would vote 'Yes'. Females were also more likely than men to be Undecided or to Abstain altogether from the Referendum.

Those who intended to vote 'No' were overwhelmingly from Santiago, the capital of the Country. Chile's geography presents a connectivity challenge. The Centre, South Centre and North Centre concentrate 10 of the 18 million inhabitants. [3]

Education - Income

A correlation between a Postsecondary Education and a 'No' vote intention can be suggested. University Students were a known faction of the opposition to Pinochet. Universities are also highly concentrated in the capital.

Highest (200.000 CLP PCM) and lowest (2500 CLP PCM) incomes are related to a 'Yes' intention of vote. This is potentially explained by the positive Economic impact Dictatorship had on the country.

An important factor in this phemonenon are the Economical sanctions imposed by the United States on the ousted socialist Government of Salvador Allende, in the context of the Cold War. The USA has since released documents where the CIA's active participation in the Coupe d'état is detailed. [4]

The minimun wage in Chile during 1988 was 16.000 Chilean Pesos (CLP), equivalent to 57.1 USD in 1988. 50 USD in 1988 had the same value of 98.5 USD of today. [5]

Age

Older respondants were less likely to declare they would vote 'No' when compared with younger ones. 'Yes' votes were however not exclusive of elder generations.
